The PT Plan: Deltas was a PT plan used in the Spring 2025 semester. The principle was that cadets would be separated into different Deltas based on their fitness level. The table below shows the arrangement of the Deltas.

Delta Score Range
Delta 1 Failing
Delta 2 75 - 85
Delta 3 85 - 90
Delta 4 90 - 95
Delta 5 90 - 95
Delta 6 95 - 100
Delta 7 95 -100


The cadet distribution ended up being roughly 15-35 cadets, based on the Delta and where we were within the semester.

Specifics of the Plan

Third PT

Delta 1 was provided with a structured third PT workout every week. This worked well.

The intent was to require a third PT for Deltas 2-5 but to make it unstructured to encourage cadets to find a form of movement that they enjoyed and build a passion for fitness. This was not successful because the PT team stopped tracking and requiring this.

Workouts

Delta 1 took a mock PT test every third PT day. After passing two tests, they were given the opportunity to move into a higher Delta.

Deltas 2-3 were supposed to be doing form checks every PT, and their workouts were supposed to be heavily centered around the elements required for the PT Test. However, the PT Commander did not require this from the PTLs, which we suspect skewed the results of our study.

Deltas 4-7 had more varied and challenging workouts. This created the opportunity to have distance runs (3 miles or more) during PT, which created an extra challenge. We would have also liked to include swimming workouts and lifting workouts but were unable to.

POC

POC were not divided into Deltas based on their score. Instead, they were distributed across all Deltas to help supervise. High-fitness-performing POC stated that they were getting some of the best workouts they have experienced during PT. Most POC were not affected by the plans, which was because we made the assumption that POC should be taking care of their own fitness.
